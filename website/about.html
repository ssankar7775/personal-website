About

Aerospace Engineer — SpaceX Falcon 9 launch operations, avionics, and systems engineering.

Introduction

Hi, I’m Tosh Sankar, an Aerospace Engineer driven by solving complex challenges and contributing to projects that push the boundaries of what's possible. Growing up near Detroit, my fascination with automotive engineering led me to aerospace after seeing SpaceX's Falcon 9 landing — and I eventually had the privilege of working on those very rockets as a Graduate Engineer at SpaceX.

In high school I developed CAD, programming, and robotics skills through IB coursework and extracurriculars. After beginning at Western Michigan University, I transferred to the University of Michigan to pursue hands-on project work and leadership opportunities.

At U‑M I joined teams such as MASA and the CubeSat Flight Lab, taking roles in design, integration, and systems engineering. These projects taught me rigorous testing, documentation, and effective team coordination. I’m currently the Avionics Systems Engineer for MERO (Michigan Experimental Rocketry Organization), architecting a flight-ready avionics system for a liquid rocket.

My background spans spacecraft subsystems, launch operations, composite manufacturing, and avionics — I enjoy turning complex requirements into practical, testable systems.

Education

I am currently pursuing a Master of Engineering in Space Systems (GPA: 4.0/4.0, Jan 2026 – May 2027 expected) at the University of Michigan’s Climate and Space Sciences and Engineering Department, with a specialization in Controls and Embedded Systems. Relevant graduate coursework includes Management of Space Systems, Control System Analysis and Design, and Astrodynamics.

I earned my Bachelor of Science and Engineering in Aerospace Engineering from the University of Michigan (Aug 2021 – Aug 2024), after initially starting my studies at Western Michigan University, where I was recognized on the Dean’s List. My undergraduate education focused on Aerodynamics, Spacecraft Dynamics, and Propulsion, complemented by hands-on experience with liquid rocketry, CubeSat development, and human-powered submarine design.

Work Experience

SpaceX — Graduate Engineer, Payload Integration & Launch Engineering

Vandenberg, CA  •  Aug 2025 – Dec 2025

  • Engineered and deployed an additional payload avionics checkout rack, increasing payload processing throughput by 50% to support increased Falcon 9 launch cadence.
  • Developed a simulation environment to validate automated payload avionics checkout sequences, enabling hardware-independent testing and engineer training.
  • Automated payload checkout test plans using Python-based tooling, reducing required engineering support by 1 hour per launch campaign.
  • Integrated and verified payload avionics hardware during Falcon 9 launch processing, supporting Transporter-15, Sentinel 6B, and over 20 Starlink missions.
